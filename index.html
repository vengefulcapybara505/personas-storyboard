Walmart Self-Checkout Personas and Storyboard

For this project, I had to pick an interface in a public place and analyze user interactions with it. I then had to conduct interviews with the various users and ask them questions about their experience. Using the data collected, I then came up with personas with characteristics I believe best fit the consumers. Using one of the personas, I then created a storyboard that showcases some of the issues with the interface. This gives us practice on analyzing existing interfaces and conducting user research to find the pros and cons of a particular design.

I chose the self-checkout station at Walmart for my interface because I have had personal difficulty using their interface before that extends further than just the software. The Walmart self-check checkout has two lower platforms for items on either side of the scanner. It allows for payment using cash and credit cards, but no contactless payments. It has a big touchscreen that shows the items paid for and bill total. Users pull up their cart or their basket and scan items on the scanner placing them onto the adjacent platform for bagging - the user can either bag while scanning or after payment. After scanning all the items, the user proceeds with payment and takes their items. The interface is trying to make it easy for a user to checkout. It needs to be easily learnable and efficient.

I conducted a set of 3 interviews of users of the kiosk.
The questions I asked them are down below:

  1. How do you as a user expect to start the checkout process?
  2. What information do you want to see on the touchscreen?
  3. How do you as user expect the payment process to work? Does it live up to your expectations?
  4. What is the easiest part about checking out with the kiosk?
  5. If you had to change anything about the kiosk, what would it be?




General observations of self-checkout users:

  • People would scan their credit card and then select payment method.
  • People often left behind their receipts
  • People would place stuff on left platform if using a cart and then set off the scale theft prevention system. Had to have help come and enter their code into kiosk. Happened multiple times
  • People only seemed to click the “finished scanning” and “select payment method” button on the kiosk. No one seemed to use the other buttons.
  • People often looked like they were in a hurry, grabbing their stuff quickly and leaving.

Based on the data and responses above,
I have created two personas I believe best represent the users I interviewed.

First, what is a persona?
User personas are typical users whose goals and characteristics represent the needs of a larger group of users. The descriptions include behavior patterns, goals, skills, attitudes, and background information, as well as the environment in which a persona exists. They often include a few fictional personal details to make the persona a realistic character as well as context-specific details (for this, it might include a persona's shopping habits and technological sophistication).

Busy Barbara

Mother of 3 children. Has to complete shopping before she must pick up her kids from school and then go home and cook while they are at sports practice. She shops for the cheapest options.


Barbara is constantly rushing her shopping and frequently triggers the scale alarm. She puts her groceries on the incorrect platform or back into the cart. She forgets her reciept behind frequently. She represents the busy mother and the other woman who triggered the alarm.

Savvy Sean

Single, techy, and young. Purchases everything with Apply Pay. Doesn't buy many items at any one time and uses self-checkout for ease of use and time efficiency.


Sean doesn't like to pay using anything besides contactless payment. He also doesn't have an option to electronically send the reciept instead of getting a paper copy. He represents the mother and the young man since they have problems with contactless payment and wish for alternative reciepts.



Storyboard for Savvy Sean

I picked the Sean as the persona for the storyboard because I believe he is the best fit user for the mass user base.

What is a storyboard?
A storyboard is a visual representation of a user's interaction with a product. It depicts a product that is similar to a film in terms of how people would utilize it. For this instance it would show how a Sean uses the self-checkout to scan his items and pay and any issues he has in doing so. The storyboard is meant to incorporate characteristics of the persona.
