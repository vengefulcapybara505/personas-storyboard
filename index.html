Walmart Self-Checkout Personas and Storyboard

I chose the self-checkout station at Walmart because I have had personal difficulty using their interface before that extends further than just the software. The Walmart self-check checkout has two lower platforms for items on either side of the scanner. It allows for payment using cash and credit cards, but no contactless payments. It has a big touchscreen that shows the items paid for and bill total. Users pull up their cart or their basket and scan items on the scanner placing them onto the adjacent platform for bagging - the user can either bag while scanning or after payment. After scanning all the items, the user proceeds with payment and takes their items. The interface is trying to make it easy for a user to checkout. It needs to be easily learnable and efficient.

I conducted a set of 3 interviews of users of the kiosk.
The questions I asked them are down below:

  1. How do you as a user expect to start the checkout process?
  2. What information do you want to see on the touchscreen?
  3. How do you as user expect the payment process to work? Does it live up to your expectations?
  4. What is the easiest part about checking out with the kiosk?
  5. If you had to change anything about the kiosk, what would it be?

General observations of self-checkout users:

  • People would scan their credit card and then select payment method.
  • People often left behind their receipts
  • People would place stuff on left platform if using a cart and then set off the scale theft prevention system. Had to have help come and enter their code into kiosk. Happened multiple times
  • People only seemed to click the “finished scanning” and “select payment method” button on the kiosk. No one seemed to use the other buttons.
  • People often looked like they were in a hurry, grabbing their stuff quickly and leaving.

Responses to interview questions:

Person 1: Mother with young child in a hurry. Used a cart.

  1. How do you as a user expect to start the checkout process?
    • Wheel cart or basket up to kiosk.
    • Start scanning items.
  2. What information do you want to see on the touchscreen?
    • Items scanned
    • Prices
    • Total and tax
    • Options for different payment methods
  3. How do you as user expect the payment process to work? Does it live up to your expectations?
    • I expect it to read my card when I tap it, but this kiosk doesn't have tap to pay.
  4. What is the easiest part about checking out with the kiosk?
    • The kiosk has two scanners one for smaller items you can carry and a handheld one for big items not to be removed from cart.
    • Lets me know when I scanned something by beeping.
  5. If you had to change anything about the kiosk, what would it be?
    • Tap-to-pay functionality

Person 2: Young well-dressed man. Seemed to have used kiosk many times before. Using a basket.

  1. How do you as a user expect to start the checkout process?
    • Scan items
  2. What information do you want to see on the touchscreen?
    • The items I am buying
  3. How do you as user expect the payment process to work? Does it live up to your expectations?
    • I use cash so I select cash and then put in the bills. It gives me my change.
    • It works fine and it's pretty easy to use.
  4. What is the easiest part about checking out with the kiosk?
    • The cash payment system. It's quick and accurate.
  5. If you had to change anything about the kiosk, what would it be?
    • An option to email receipts would be helpful.

Person 3: Middle aged woman. Cart was full.

  1. How do you as a user expect to start the checkout process?
    • Bring up the cart and scan my items
  2. What information do you want to see on the touchscreen?
    • Total prices and items scanned
    • Payment methods for card or cash
  3. How do you as user expect the payment process to work? Does it live up to your expectations?
    • When I'm done, I press done scanning and then swipe my credit card.
    • No, it didn't because this kiosk for some reason made me select my payment method even though it told me my card was read. I shouldn't have to click after the fact.
  4. What is the easiest part about checking out with the kiosk?
    • The scanner is quick.
    • The big touchscreen with big buttons is easy to use. I have used ones with small buttons and they are hard to use.
  5. If you had to change anything about the kiosk, what would it be?
    • The platform on the left doesn't have a scale and when I am scanning things from the cart, I subconsciously place things there and then it triggers the alarm.
    • I am not really sure what the point of the scale is because they have other theft prevention measures in place.

Based on the data and responses above,
I have created two personas I believe best represent the users I interviewed.

Busy Barbara

Mother of 3 children. Has to complete shopping before she must pick up her kids from school and then go home and cook while they are at sports practice. She shops for the cheapest options.

Barbara is constantly rushing her shopping and frequently triggers the scale alarm. She puts her groceries on the incorrect platform or back into the cart. She forgets her reciept behind frequently. She represents the busy mother and the other woman who triggered the alarm.

Savvy Sean

Single, techy, and young. Purchases everything with Apply Pay. Doesn't buy many items at any one time and uses self-checkout for ease of use and time efficiency.

Sean doesn't like to pay using anything besides contactless payment. He also doesn't have an option to electronically send the reciept instead of getting a paper copy. He represents the mother and the young man since they have problems with contactless payment and wish for alternative reciepts.
